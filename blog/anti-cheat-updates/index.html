Anti-Cheat Updates: What You Need to Know

Posted on July 30, 2025 by COD Refill Team

Cheating in online games ruins the fun for everyone, and Call of Duty is no exception. That’s why the developers have rolled out a major update to the RICOCHET Anti-Cheat system for Season 05. These new changes are designed to create a more balanced, fair, and competitive environment — whether you’re dropping into Warzone or grinding in multiplayer.

Key Changes

  • Enhanced Detection: Improved scanning algorithms to detect aimbots, wallhacks, and input manipulation faster and more accurately.
  • Real-Time Monitoring: Player reports now trigger faster investigations with immediate in-match monitoring.
  • Ban Wave Improvements: Increased frequency of ban waves to quickly remove cheaters before they disrupt more matches.
  • Replay Review System: New replay tools for developers and moderators to review suspicious plays frame-by-frame.
  • Stronger Hardware Bans: Bans tied to hardware IDs to stop repeat offenders from simply creating new accounts.

What This Means for Players

With these updates, legitimate players should notice cleaner lobbies and fewer unfair deaths. It’s still important to use the in-game Report Player option if you see suspicious behavior — every report helps the system improve. Remember, the goal is to make every gunfight about skill, not cheats.

Tips to Stay Safe

  • Never download third-party software claiming to improve performance — it could get you banned.
  • Use official game clients and avoid “modded” versions of the game.
  • Keep your account secure with two-factor authentication.
  • Regularly check the official patch notes for security updates.

Final Thoughts

Fair play is at the heart of the Call of Duty community. With the latest RICOCHET updates, the fight against cheaters is stronger than ever. Whether you’re a casual player or a competitive grinder, these changes are here to make every match more enjoyable and competitive.
